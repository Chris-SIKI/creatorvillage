The Process

How it works.

From land acquisition to move-in day, Cleado geodesic domes go from delivery to livable home in just 12 hours — with built-in solar and zero cost to residents.

1
Acquire Land
Land obtained through donation, government surplus programs, or below-market purchase. Cleado domes require no heavy foundations, reducing site prep costs.
2
Deploy Cleado Domes
Patented lightweight geodesic dome kits arrive flat-packed and are assembled on-site in 12 hours. No heavy machinery or specialized labor required.
3
Instant Solar Power
Each dome includes 10 m² of built-in thin-film solar PV generating ~5,000 kWh/year, with battery storage for fully off-grid operation from day one.
4
CLT Ownership
Housing owned by Creator Village Community Land Trust. Residents receive immediate ownership of their home through a 99-year renewable ground lease at $0/month.
5
Zero-Cost Living
No rent, no mortgage, no energy bills. Residents contribute through content creation and community participation: maintenance, governance, or mentoring.

Ownership Model

Community Land Trust
model.

The CLT separates land ownership from housing use, ensuring permanent affordability. Land is held in trust; residents get lifetime security without the burden of a mortgage.

1
Nonprofit CLT Acquires Land
Creator Village CLT purchases or receives donated land, removing it permanently from the speculative market.
2
CLT Retains Permanent Ownership
The nonprofit holds title to the land forever. It can never be sold to private developers or speculators.
3
99-Year Ground Lease Issued
Residents receive a renewable, inheritable ground lease providing full security of tenure.
4
$0 Monthly Ground Lease Fee
Creator Village model charges $0/month. Typical CLTs charge $10-$75/month. Creator Village eliminates even that.
5
Resale Restrictions
If a resident sells improvements, resale formula maintains affordability for the next resident permanently.
6
Unit Returns to Trust
When a resident leaves, the unit returns to the CLT to house the next qualifying household.

Low-Income Housing Tax Credit for new construction. The most powerful federal affordable housing tool. Competitive allocation.
LIHTC 4% Credit
Up to 30% subsidy
Non-competitive LIHTC allocation paired with tax-exempt bonds. Easier to obtain than the 9% credit.
LIHTC 2026 Expansion
Permanent 12% increase
Starting 2026, permanent 12% increase in state allocation of LIHTC credits. More housing credits available than ever before.
USDA Section 502
Direct loans, low/very-low income
Rural housing direct loans for low and very-low income households. Below-market interest rates, up to 33-year terms.
USDA Section 515
Rural rental housing
Low-interest loans for constructing or improving rural rental housing for low-income, elderly, or disabled residents.

Revenue Engine

House sponsorships.

Every home in Creator Village is tied to a creator and their brand partnerships. Brands sponsor individual houses — paying for the creator’s living costs in exchange for authentic content from the community. As sponsorship revenue grows, residents’ $1,000/mo cost drops to zero. Three homes serve as main community centers for shared studios, events, and collaboration spaces.

1
Creators Move In
100 creators across all tiers — from nano to established — move into Creator Village at $1,000/mo. Three additional homes are designated as community centers: a shared production studio, an event space, and a collaboration hub.
2
Brand Sponsorships
Creator Village negotiates house sponsorships with brands. Each creator’s home becomes a branded content hub — brands get access to authentic creators living in a visually stunning community. Sponsorship value scales with follower count.
3
Content & Campaigns
Creators produce sponsored content, product integrations, and brand campaigns directly from their homes. The community itself is the set — every piece of content showcases the Creator Village lifestyle.
4
Cost Drops to Zero
As house sponsorship revenue exceeds the $1,000/mo baseline, residents’ costs are eliminated. The goal: every creator living completely free, funded entirely by the brands that sponsor their house.
5
Scale & Replicate
Surplus revenue funds new Creator Village communities. The model is self-replicating — each community proves the sponsorship economics and attracts the next wave of brands and creators.

Why This Model Works
Creators spend 50–70% of their income on housing, energy, and internet. Creator Village eliminates these three biggest costs, meaning creators keep far more of their earnings while producing more content. The 30% community share replaces costs they were already paying — and funds the entire community operation. For brands, a village of 100 creators living in visually stunning geodesic domes is an irresistible marketing opportunity that no individual influencer can offer.
