The Process

How it works.

From land acquisition to move-in day, Cleado geodesic domes go from delivery to livable home in just 12 hours — with built-in solar and off-grid energy from day one.

1
Acquire Land
Land obtained through donation, government surplus programs, or below-market purchase. Cleado domes require no heavy foundations, reducing site prep costs.
2
Deploy Cleado Domes
Patented lightweight geodesic dome kits arrive flat-packed and are assembled on-site in 12 hours. No heavy machinery or specialized labor required.
3
Instant Solar Power
Each dome includes built-in thin-film solar PV generating 7,000–9,000 kWh/year, with battery storage for fully off-grid operation from day one.
4
CLT Ownership
Housing owned by Creator Village Community Land Trust. Residents receive their home through a 99-year renewable ground lease — ensuring long-term security and affordability.
5
Community Living
Brand partnerships help offset costs for residents. Creators contribute through content creation and community participation — collaboration, governance, and mentoring.

Housing + Energy = One Solution
Traditional approaches require separate budgets and contractors for housing and solar. Cleado eliminates this by integrating thin-film solar directly into the dome structure. How It’s Funded

Brand partnerships.

Brand sponsorships help fund the community. Companies partner with Creator Village to have their products and branding integrated into creator homes — in exchange for authentic, daily content from 95+ creators living in a visually stunning community.

1
Creators Move In
95+ creators across all tiers — from nano to established — move into Creator Village. Three community centers provide shared production studios, event spaces, and collaboration hubs.
2
Brands Sponsor Homes
Creator Village partners with brands who sponsor individual domes. Each creator’s home becomes a branded content hub — brands get access to authentic creators living in a community built for content.
3
Content & Campaigns
Creators produce content, product integrations, and brand campaigns directly from their homes. The community itself is the set — every piece of content showcases the Creator Village lifestyle.
4
Community Revenue
Sponsorship revenue helps cover operating costs — housing, energy, internet, studios, staffing, and maintenance. The more the community grows, the more costs are offset for residents.
5
Scale & Replicate
Surplus revenue funds new Creator Village communities. The model is self-sustaining — each community proves the economics and attracts the next wave of brands and creators.
